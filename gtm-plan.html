Part 1 — The 5 Pillars
What MDB installs for Algomarketing

Each pillar addresses a specific structural gap identified in the Algomarketing research. Taken together, they replace referral-dependent growth with a systematic enterprise acquisition channel.

Pillar 1

Enterprise Account Database

30 scored and ranked enterprise accounts across Algomarketing's Ideal Client Profile. Buying signal data (new CMO hiring, transformation programme signals, headcount freeze announcements) included for each account. Updated monthly.

30 accounts Week 1 to 2 Owned permanently
Pillar 2

Personalised Outreach Sequences

2-sequence personalised outreach for each account segment. Email 1: Trigger angle (new CMO window). Email 2: Friction angle (HelloKindred vs Algomarketing authority gap). Each sequence tested for PSS score 85 or above before sending.

2 sequences per segment PSS 85+ required Week 2 to 4
Pillar 3

Authority Engine (Content + Nurture)

LinkedIn content flywheel targeting enterprise CMO pain topics (AI marketing talent, headcount freeze workarounds, embedded versus permanent trade-offs). 20-week email nurture sequence for opted-in prospects. Built once, runs permanently.

Weekly LinkedIn posts 20-week nurture Week 3 to 5
Pillar 4

Micro-Budget Retargeting

£5 to £10 per day LinkedIn retargeting to re-engage anyone who has visited the Algomarketing website, opened a nurture email, or engaged with a LinkedIn post in the last 30 days. Keeps Algomarketing visible in enterprise CMO feeds between active touchpoints.

£5 to £10 per day LinkedIn feed Week 4 onwards
Pillar 5

Lead Scoring and Hot Prospect Alerts

Cold, warm, and hot bucket system wired into Algomarketing's CRM. Hot criteria: visited the services page or pricing page, opened 3 or more nurture emails, and engaged with a LinkedIn post in the last 30 days. Oliver's commercial team receives an alert only when a prospect crosses the threshold — not for every engagement signal. Means the sales team focuses on intent, not noise.

3-tier scoring CRM-wired alerts Week 4 to 6 Owned permanently
Part 2 — The 10-Day Outreach Sequence
How Algomarketing reaches Oliver Hassell

The day-by-day multi-channel sequence MDB uses to open enterprise conversations. Each step is distinct. No repetition. No generic follow-ups.

Day 0
Email 1 — Trigger Angle Email
Subject: "New CMO window" — PSS 88. Trigger email referencing the 30-company enterprise target list. Delivery CTA: "I can send the list over." References specific companies in an active evaluation window. 103 words.
Day 1
LinkedIn Connection Request LinkedIn
Personalised connection note: references the email sent yesterday. Short — 2 sentences. References Algomarketing's 10-year track record and the authority gap finding. No pitch in the note itself.
Day 3
LinkedIn Direct Message (if connected) LinkedIn
If Oliver accepted the connection: short DM referencing the research package. "Built the full enterprise target list and an Authority Engine diagnosis specifically for Algomarketing — happy to share both." Link to the GitHub Pages package.
Day 5
Email 2 — Friction Angle Email
Subject: "HelloKindred vs Algomarketing" — PSS 86. Friction angle comparing HelloKindred's content authority against Algomarketing's gap. Asset CTA: "Built the target list already: [LINK]." 112 words. Different angle from Email 1 — competitive gap rather than timing trigger.
Day 7
LinkedIn Post Engagement LinkedIn
If Oliver has posted anything in the last 30 days: a substantive comment (2 to 3 sentences, adds a specific observation or data point). Not a vanity like. Not "Great post." This is about visibility in Oliver's notifications before the final follow-up.
Day 10
LinkedIn Breakup Message LinkedIn
Short final message. "Last one from me — if the timing is wrong, I understand completely. The enterprise target list and the Authority Engine diagnosis are there when you want them. Here is the link: [URL]." No ask. Just access.
