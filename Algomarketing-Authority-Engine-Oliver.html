Section 1
The Situation

Algomarketing has built a remarkable business over 10 years. Google, Zoom, Stripe, Cisco, OpenAI — these are not small wins. They represent a track record that most marketing staffing firms would spend decades chasing.

But every one of those clients came through relationships and referrals.

The JD for the Senior Growth Manager role says it directly: "Over the past 10 years, Algo has grown organically through referrals, relationships, and reputation."

That is not a criticism. It is a description of a very common pattern for Business to Business service firms at Algomarketing's stage.

The challenge is what happens next.

Section 2
The Authority Gap

Enterprise marketing leaders researching embedded AI marketing talent in 2026 conduct the same research journey as any other major procurement decision. They Google the firm. They check LinkedIn. They look for case studies, testimonials, and thought leadership that tells them whether this partner understands their world.

What they find when they search for Algomarketing is a professional website, solid delivery proof, and a client logo bar.

What they also find — when they search for "enterprise embedded marketing talent UK" or "AI-proficient marketing specialists" — is HelloKindred's industry reports, Stopgap's blog content, and Major Players' brand recognition.

This is the authority gap. Algomarketing wins when it gets in the room. The authority gap means the room fills with competitors before Algomarketing is invited.
Section 3
What Algomarketing's Enterprise Buyers Need to See

Enterprise Business to Business buyers conduct 13 digital touchpoints before engaging a new service provider. Those touchpoints happen across LinkedIn, organic search, email, and peer networks.

Algomarketing is present at touchpoint 13: the referral. It is absent at touchpoints 1 through 12.

The authority infrastructure that fills those 12 touchpoints is not complex. It is systematic. And its absence is costing Algomarketing enterprise mandates it would win if it could get in the room — from companies like XTM International, ComplyAdvantage, Personio, Quantexa, and Darktrace. All active buyers in Algomarketing's Ideal Client Profile. None of them currently in a conversation with Algomarketing's team.

Section 4
The Three Signals That Matter

The authority gap is not caused by poor content quality. Algomarketing's existing case studies are credible. The AI-proficient embedded model is a genuine differentiation story.

The gap is caused by three structural absences:

Signal 1 — Missing

No Content Flywheel

Enterprise CMOs and VP Marketing leaders need to encounter Algomarketing content regularly — not when they are actively searching for a supplier, but when they are solving marketing problems in their day-to-day. A content flywheel means posts, articles, and case studies showing up in their feed consistently. The audience grows. The authority compounds.

Signal 2 — Missing

No Nurture Infrastructure

The enterprise procurement cycle for a major staffing relationship runs 6 to 18 months. A prospect who encounters Algomarketing in month 1 but is not in a buying window until month 12 needs something to bridge that gap. Without a nurture sequence, the prospect forgets. When the buying window opens, they call whoever is most present in their memory.

Signal 3 — Missing

No Warm-to-Hot Signal Tracking

Enterprise marketing leaders who have engaged with Algomarketing content, attended an event, or replied to a cold email are warm prospects. Without a lead scoring system, they are indistinguishable from cold prospects. When the buying window opens, the sales team does not know who to prioritise.

Section 5
What the Authority Engine Looks Like for Algomarketing

This is what MDB would install — specifically for Algomarketing's enterprise audience:

01

LinkedIn Content Flywheel (weeks 1 to 4: strategy and calendar built, running from week 5)

Weekly posts on topics enterprise CMOs care about: AI marketing tools, headcount-free capability scaling, platform migration challenges, embedded talent versus agency trade-offs. Posts are buyer-useful observations — not company announcements. Target audience: CMOs, VP Marketing, Marketing Directors at enterprise technology and financial services companies. Output: growing LinkedIn audience of the exact buyers Algomarketing wants to reach.

02

20-Week Email Nurture Sequence (built once, runs permanently)

20 emails over 5 months to opted-in enterprise marketing leaders. Zero sales pitches. Pure value delivery: how to spec a contingent marketing brief, what AI proficiency means in a 2026 marketing team, how to manage contractor compliance across jurisdictions. Week 21: the first commercial email. The subscriber has received 20 pieces of value. The call to action is earned. Output: 150 to 500 warm prospects in a nurture sequence within 12 months.

03

Micro-Budget Retargeting (£5 to £10 per day on LinkedIn)

Retargets anyone who has visited the Algomarketing website, engaged with a LinkedIn post, or opened a nurture email. Keeps Algomarketing visible to warm prospects between active touches. Costs less than one coffee per day per prospect. Output: Algomarketing appears in the LinkedIn feeds of the exact people most likely to buy.

04

Lead Scoring System (installed in parallel with outbound channel)

Categorises the email list and LinkedIn audience into cold, warm, and hot. Hot criteria: visited the pricing or services page plus opened 3 nurture emails plus engaged with a LinkedIn post in the last 30 days. Sales team alerted only when a prospect crosses the threshold. Output: Oliver's commercial team focuses on prospects with demonstrated intent, not the whole list.
