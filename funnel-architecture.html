The Leak Map
Where Algomarketing loses enterprise mandates it would win

The AAA Diagnostic (Attraction, Alignment, Activation) identifies the four primary leaks. Each leak is a mandate lost before the first conversation happens.

Attraction — Top of Funnel
Leak 1: Invisible at the Research Stage
Enterprise CMOs researching embedded marketing talent suppliers conduct 13 digital touchpoints before engaging a new partner. Algomarketing is present at touchpoint 13 (the referral). It is absent at touchpoints 1 through 12. Every mandate that begins with a Google search or a LinkedIn discovery is decided before Algomarketing enters the room.
LinkedIn content flywheel + organic search presence via consistent long-form posts targeting enterprise CMO search terms.
Attraction — New CMO Window
Leak 2: Missing the Highest-Value Procurement Trigger
When a new CMO or VP Marketing joins a target enterprise account, they restructure their preferred supplier list in the first 30 to 90 days. This is the window when a firm with no prior relationship can get shortlisted. Algomarketing is not systematically monitoring this trigger across its 30 target accounts — missing the best timing window in enterprise procurement.
Buying signal tracking system flags new marketing leadership appointments across the 30-company target list within 48 hours of announcement.
Alignment — Middle of Funnel
Leak 3: Non-Active Prospects Forget Algomarketing Exists
The enterprise procurement cycle for a major staffing relationship runs 6 to 18 months. A prospect who has one conversation in month 1 but is not in a buying window until month 12 needs something to bridge that gap. Without a nurture sequence, the prospect forgets. When the buying window opens, they call whoever has stayed present — which is usually HelloKindred.
20-week email nurture sequence (value-only, no pitches) keeps Algomarketing present with non-active prospects for 5 months. Retargeting extends visibility for another 30 to 90 days beyond that.
Activation — Warm Prospects
Leak 4: Warm Prospects Indistinguishable from Cold
Enterprise marketing leaders who have engaged with Algomarketing content, attended an event, or replied to a cold email are warm prospects. Without a lead scoring system, they are indistinguishable from cold prospects. When the buying window opens, the commercial team does not know who to prioritise — so they work the full list, missing the warm signals that indicate readiness to engage.
Lead scoring system categorises the database into cold, warm, and hot. Hot prospect alerts notify Oliver's team only when a prospect has demonstrated clear intent — visited services page, opened 3 or more nurture emails, and engaged with a LinkedIn post in the last 30 days.
The 3 Growth Loops
How the infrastructure compounds over time

Each loop feeds the next. The first is the core acquisition loop. The second is the credibility and case study loop. The third is the test, measure, learn, and scale loop.

Loop 1 — Core Acquisition
Outbound to Mandate to Referral
  • → Enterprise account database signals a buying window (new CMO hire)
  • → Personalised outreach sequence sent — Trigger angle, timing-specific
  • → Conversation booked, proposal presented, mandate won
  • → Delivery quality turns the new client into a reference
  • → Reference generates a referral to a second enterprise account
  • → Referral adds a verified contact to the database for the next outreach cycle
  • → Loop compounds: each mandate strengthens the proof base for the next
Loop 2 — Credibility Engine
Delivery to Case Study to Inbound
  • → Mandate delivered — specific results documented (time to deploy, output metrics, cost vs permanent hire)
  • → Case study extracted and published — LinkedIn post, website case study page
  • → Enterprise CMO reading the case study recognises their own situation
  • → Organic inbound inquiry — they reach out before being outreached to
  • → New mandate won from inbound — zero outbound cost for this account
  • → Inbound account becomes a new case study — loop repeats at higher authority level
  • → HelloKindred's authority gap narrows as Algomarketing's published proof base grows
Loop 3 — Test and Scale
Signal to Learning to Better Signal
  • → Outreach sequences deployed across 30 accounts in two segments
  • → Email open rates, reply rates, and conversion rates measured per segment
  • → Highest-performing angle identified (Trigger vs Friction vs Comparison)
  • → Winning angle scaled to the next 30-account batch
  • → Lead scoring data identifies which account characteristics produce the hottest prospects
  • → Ideal Client Profile refined — tighter targeting, higher reply rates
  • → Each 30-day cycle produces better signals than the last — channel gets sharper, not weaker
